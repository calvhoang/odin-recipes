Kimbap Recipe

Kimbap also known as gimbap, is a seaweed rice roll. Gim meaning seaweed and bap meaning rice. It is usually filled with a variety of vegetables such as carrot, spinach, pickled radish, etc and protein such as eggs, beef (bulgogi), etc.

Instructions

  • 5 sheets of gim (seaweed paper), roasted slightly
  • 4 cups of cooked rice (make 2 cups of uncooked short grain rice)
  • 1/2 pound beef skirt steak (alternatively, tenderlion or ground beef)
  • 1 large carrot, cut into matchsticks (about 1 1/2 cup)
  • 5 strips of yellow pickled radish, 8 inch long strips
  • 8 to 10 ounces of spinach, blanched and rinsed in cold water then strained
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tps soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp and 1 tps of brown or white sugar
  • 1 1/2 tps kosher salt
  • 2 1/2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • vegetable oil
